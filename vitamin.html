VITAMIN BENEFITS DEFICIENCIES SOURCES
Vitamin A
  • Vision: aids in low-light and color vision.
  • Immune Function: development and activity of white blood cells for immune response.
  • Cell Growth and Differentiation: Regulates the growth and specialization of cells.
  • Skin Health: Contributes to the maintenance and repair of skin and mucous membranes.
  • Reproductive Health: Plays a role in reproductive processes and fetal development.
  • Eye disorder (xerophthalmia, night blindness, corneal ulceration, and irreversible blindness).
  • Growth problems in children, poor wound healing, and dry, bumpy skin rashes (follicular hyperkeratosis).
Vitamin B1 (Thiamin)
  • Energy Metabolism: Crucial for converting carbohydrates into energy, helping to produce ATP (adenosine triphosphate).
  • Nervous System Function: Supports proper nerve function by aiding in the synthesis of neurotransmitters.
  • Muscle Function: Plays a role in muscle contraction and overall muscle health.
  • Metabolism of Other Nutrients: Involved in the metabolism of proteins and fats, facilitating the use of various nutrients.
  • Cognitive Function: Supports brain health and cognitive processes, contributing to memory and concentration.
  • Cardiovascular Health: Helps maintain healthy heart function by supporting energy production in cardiac muscle cells.

Vitamin B2 (Riboflavin)
  • Energy Production: Essential for converting carbohydrates, fats, and proteins into energy by participating in the electron transport chain.
  • Antioxidant Function: Helps protect cells from oxidative stress by supporting the function of antioxidants like glutathione.
  • Metabolism of Other Nutrients: Aids in the metabolism of other B vitamins, including B6 and folate.
  • Skin Health: Contributes to maintaining healthy skin, eyes, and mucous membranes.
  • Red Blood Cell Production: Plays a role in the formation of red blood cells, which are crucial for oxygen transport in the body.
  • Growth and Development: Supports normal growth and development, particularly in infants and children.
  • Not getting enough of the vitamin from the diet due to the affect absorption in the intestine, or increase in the excretion of the vitamin from the body.
  • Signs & Symptoms: cracked and red lops, inflammation of the lining of the mouth and tongue, mouth ulcers, cracks at the corners of the mouth, and sore throat.
  • Dry and scaling skin, fluid and mucous membranes, and iron-deficiency anemia. Eyes are bloodshot, itchy, watery, and sensitive to bright light.
